What is AIDS?

AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome) is a chronic, potentially life-threatening condition caused by the human immunodeficiency virus (HIV). By damaging your immune system, HIV interferes with your body's ability to fight infection and disease.

Causes of AIDS

AIDS is caused by HIV. The virus can be transmitted through contact with infected blood, semen, or vaginal fluids.

Symptoms of AIDS

People with AIDS may experience symptoms such as rapid weight loss, recurring fever, extreme tiredness, and prolonged swelling of the lymph glands.

Prevention Methods

Prevention methods include using protection during sexual activities, not sharing needles, and getting tested regularly for HIV.

Find Support

If you or someone you know is affected by AIDS, support is available. Reach out to local health organizations, clinics, and support groups for help.
