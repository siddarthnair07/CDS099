Causes of AIDS

  • Acquired immunodeficiency syndrome (AIDS) is caused by the human immunodeficiency virus (HIV).

  • HIV is spread through certain body fluids, and the most common ways to contract it are:

    • 1.Unprotected sex: Having vaginal or anal sex without a condom, especially receptive anal sex.
    2.Sharing needles: Sharing needles, syringes, or other drug injection equipment.
    3.Pregnancy and breastfeeding: Transmission from mother to baby during pregnancy, birth, or breastfeeding.
    4.Other high-risk behaviors: Having multiple sexual partners, having a sexually transmitted infection (STI), or using drugs or alcohol in a sexual context.
    5.Unsafe medical procedures: Receiving unsafe injections, blood transfusions, or tissue transplantation.
    6.Accidental needle stick injuries: Getting accidentally stuck by a needle, including health workers.

    HIV is a fragile virus that doesn't survive outside the body for long. It can't be transmitted through sweat, urine, or saliva. If HIV is controlled, the risk of transmission is close to zero.

    There are powerful tools to help prevent HIV transmission, including:
  • Using a new condom correctly every time you have sex.
  • Taking pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP), a combination of HIV drugs taken before potential exposure to HIV.
  • Taking post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP), medication taken after an exposure to prevent getting HIV.
