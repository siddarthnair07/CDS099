Symptoms of AIDS

Symptoms of AIDS include:

  • Rapid weight loss
  • Recurring fever or night sweats
  • Extreme tiredness
  • Swollen lymph glands in the armpits, groin, or neck
  • Diarrhea that lasts for more than a week
  • Sores of the mouth, anus, or genitals
  • Pneumonia
  • Lung infections
  • Intestinal infections
  • Rashes

AIDS occurs when HIV weakens the body's immune system and the infection progresses. HIV can be treated with antiretroviral therapy (ART), which can keep blood levels undetectable. If you get ART early and stay on it, you can live a long and healthy life.


Many people have no symptoms when they are first infected with HIV.


The first signs of HIV infection may be flu-like symptoms, such as: Fever, Chills, Rash, Muscle aches, Sore throat, Fatigue, Swollen lymph nodes, and Mouth ulcers.

You should still take an HIV test if you may have been at risk at any time in the past, even if you do not experience any symptoms.
