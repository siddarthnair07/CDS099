What is AIDS?

There are many ways to prevent HIV and AIDS, including:

  • Condoms:
    •  Use condoms every time you have sex.
  • Limited sexual partners:
    •  Have fewer sexual partners.
  • Get tested:
    •  Get tested for HIV and sexually transmitted infections (STIs).
  • Pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP):
    •  Take PrEP as prescribed to reduce the risk of getting HIV from sex by about 99%. PrEP is less effective if not taken as prescribed.
  • Avoid injecting drugs:
    •  Do not inject drugs.
  • Harm reduction services:
    •  Use harm reduction services if you inject or use drugs.
  • Male circumcision:
    •  Get a voluntary medical male circumcision.
  • Antenatal screening:
    •  If you are pregnant, get tested for HIV as part of routine antenatal screening.

    Other ways to reduce the risk of HIV include:
  • Choosing less risky sexual behaviors.
  • Getting tested and treated for STIs.
  • Talking to your health care provider about PrEP.
  • Having only low-risk sex.
  • Only having partners with the same HIV status.
  • Not having sex.
